Recipe Name:

Pilau

Description

East African Pilau is a traditional, beautiful fragrant rice dish made with many aromatic spices that adds an amazing depth of flavor to the rice. It can be made with either beef or chicken.

Recipe category:

Lunch/Dinner

Difficulty level:

Easy

Servings:

3-4

Cooking time:

35 Minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, or ghee
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes translucent and slightly golden.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot and cook for another minute, stirring continuously.
  4. Add the cumin seeds, ground coriander, turmeric powder, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaf to the pot. Stir the spices with the onion, garlic, and ginger mixture for about a minute, allowing the flavors to release and become fragrant.
  5. Add the drained basmati rice to the pot and gently stir to coat the rice grains with the spice mixture. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, to lightly toast the rice.
  6. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and add salt to taste. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Allow the rice to simmer undisturbed for about 15-20 minutes or until the liquid is fully absorbed and the rice is tender.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for about 5 minutes. This helps the rice to steam and become fluffy.
  9. Remove the lid, fluff the rice gently with a fork, and discard the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaf.
  10. Serve the pilau rice hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or parsley if desired. It pairs well with a variety of dishes such as curries, grilled meats, or vegetable stir-fries.

Additional Notes:

  • Basmati rice takes about 20 minutes to cook however, your cooking time may vary depending on the type of rice you use.
  • The ratio of rice to liquid is essential for perfectly cooked pilau. In this recipe, a ratio of 1:2 Rice:Water was used. If you prefer a softer or stickier texture, you can increase the liquid slightly.
  • After the rice is cooked, let it rest for a few minutes with the lid on. This allows the rice to steam and become fluffy.
